# Dunder Mifflin Employee Handbook  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025  
**Version:** 1.0  

Welcome to Dunder Mifflin Paper Company!  
We’re thrilled to have you as part of the team that keeps businesses stocked with the highest-quality paper — and the highest concentration of personality per square foot in Scranton.

---

## 1. Company Overview

**Mission:**  
To provide quality paper and outstanding service while fostering an environment where employees feel valued, inspired, and occasionally filmed.

**Values:**  
- Integrity in every sale.  
- Humor in every day.  
- Respect for colleagues, clients, and regional managers.  
- Commitment to sustainability and community.

---

## 2. Code of Conduct

- Treat everyone with courtesy and professionalism.  
- Harassment, discrimination, or pranks that cross the line (see *Jim vs Dwight Clause*) are prohibited.  
- Keep confidential information — including client lists and chili recipes — secure.  
- Report safety issues or policy violations to HR (currently: Toby Flenderson).  
- No weapons, fireworks, or livestock on company property without regional manager approval.

---

## 3. Working Hours & Attendance

- Standard office hours: **9 AM – 5 PM, Monday – Friday.**  
- Flexible arrangements may be approved by management.  
- Notify your supervisor before absences or lateness.  
- Excessive tardiness may result in disciplinary action (or awkward conference-room meetings).

---

## 4. Dress Code

- Business casual is standard; formal attire for client meetings or corporate visits.  
- Theme days (e.g., *Hawaiian Shirt Friday*) require tasteful participation.  
- Costumes must be workplace-appropriate — yes, even on Halloween.

---

## 5. Compensation & Benefits

### Pay
- Employees are paid bi-weekly via direct deposit.  
- Overtime must be pre-approved by management.  

### Benefits
- Health, dental, and vision insurance available to full-time employees.  
- 401(k) with company match up to 4%.  
- Paid holidays, vacation, and sick leave per policy.  
- Employee discount on all Dunder Mifflin products (paper only; printers not included).

---

## 6. Time Off

| Type | Accrual / Allocation | Notes |
|------|----------------------|-------|
| Vacation | 15 days / year | Increases with tenure |
| Sick Leave | 10 days / year | May require documentation |
| Bereavement | Up to 3 days | Per event |
| Jury Duty | Paid time off | Proof required |

Unused vacation may roll over up to 5 days per year.

---

## 7. Office Etiquette

- Keep shared areas clean (especially the kitchen microwave).  
- Label all food items — unmarked items are considered fair game after 48 hours.  
- Avoid loud phone calls or speakerphone use in open areas.  
- Pranks must be harmless, reversible, and HR-approved (good luck with that).  

---

## 8. Technology & Security

- Use company devices primarily for work purposes.  
- Protect your login credentials and lock screens when away.  
- Do not install unauthorized software.  
- Phishing emails: report to IT Support (ask Ryan, if he’s still in that department).  
- Cloud storage and collaboration tools must comply with corporate data policies.

---

## 9. Health & Safety

- Follow building safety procedures and evacuation routes.  
- Report hazards or injuries immediately.  
- Maintain ergonomic workstation setups.  
- Participation in the annual fire-drill (and optional safety skit) is mandatory.

---

## 10. Performance & Development

- Formal performance reviews occur annually.  
- Constructive feedback is encouraged; inappropriate feedback is… less encouraged.  
- Opportunities for advancement are merit-based and occasionally based on proximity to Michael Scott when he’s making decisions.  

---

## 11. Discipline & Termination

Violations of policy may result in:
1. Verbal warning  
2. Written warning  
3. Final warning or suspension  
4. Termination  

Serious offenses (e.g., property damage, fraud, or burning your foot on a George Foreman grill) may lead to immediate dismissal.

---

## 12. Equal Opportunity Statement

Dunder Mifflin is an equal-opportunity employer.  
We value diversity and inclusion, and we don’t discriminate based on race, gender, age, religion, orientation, or office branch.

---

By continuing employment with Dunder Mifflin, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agreed to abide by this handbook.
